02The Inspiration

A fragrance for the seventeen minutes the world catches fire.

"Most cologne is composed for the room. MERIDIAN is composed for the air outside it — thinner, colder, holding the day's last colour."

In 2024, Élise Marchand spent four nights at 3,200 metres in the Bernese Alps with a notebook, a clean rebreather, and twenty-two raw materials. She was looking for the smell of the moment the sun turns the snow the colour of a struck match — neither morning nor night, neither hot nor cold.

The result is MERIDIAN: a structured Eau de Parfum at 22% concentration, cold-distilled over six weeks, rested in oak for three. The composition is unusually mineral. There is bergamot, yes, and an amber base you would expect from any serious house — but threaded between them is a note we call iron oxide: the smell of stone that has held the sun all day and is just beginning to release it.

The bottle was cut to match. Fifteen facets, each angled to refract a different fraction of the spectrum. Held to a window at the right hour, it throws the same colours the perfume describes.

It is, by design, a fragrance you wear once a day at most. Twice if the day is long enough.

04How It's Worn

Three small rituals, written by the people who tested it.

i.

One spray, sternum.

Then wait. The opening reads thin for forty seconds. That is the bergamot air-drying. After it, the iron oxide warms against skin temperature.

ii.

Cold collar.

One spray on the inside of a coat collar before stepping outside. The fragrance changes when air does.

iii.

Held, not sprayed.

For evenings indoors, depress and release once into the palm. Press, do not rub. Cup briefly. Wear.
