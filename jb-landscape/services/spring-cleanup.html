Spring Cleanup Across Rhode Island

Leaves out. Beds prepped. Season-ready.

Leaf removal, bed prep, edging, dethatching, and a fresh start for the season. The reset that makes weekly service look great all summer.

Free Estimate (401) 230-4820

Overview.

A real spring cleanup is the difference between a lawn that struggles through July and one that fills in by mid-May. We pull last year’s debris off the property and prep the beds before mulch goes down.

What's included

  • Leaf and debris pickup, lawn and beds
  • Dead-stem cutback in perennial beds
  • Light dethatch of the lawn surface
  • Hard-edge along every bed and walkway
  • Fertilizer and lime applied if needed
  • All debris hauled away — nothing left curbside

Add-ons

  • Mulch installation (most popular pairing)
  • Overseeding thin areas
  • Shrub trimming
  • Pre-emergent crabgrass treatment

When to book.

We start spring cleanups as soon as the ground thaws — typically mid-March in southern Rhode Island, early April north of Providence. Book early; the calendar fills before the snow is even gone.

Why it matters in Rhode Island.

A New England winter leaves a mess that smothers a lawn if it sits. Matted leaves, fallen branches, and salt-burned edges trap moisture and block the spring growth your turf needs to fill in. A real spring cleanup pulls all of that off the property before mulch goes down, so the lawn greens up by mid-May instead of struggling into July. Booked early, it's the single best thing you can do to set up the whole season.

Frequently asked questions.

When should spring cleanup happen?

As soon as the ground thaws — typically mid-March in southern Rhode Island and early April north of Providence. Booking early matters; the calendar fills before the snow is gone.

What's included in a spring cleanup?

Leaf and debris pickup across lawn and beds, dead-stem cutback, a light dethatch, hard-edging along every bed and walk, and full haul-away — nothing left curbside.

Should I add mulch at the same time?

It's the most popular pairing. Cleaning the beds and laying fresh mulch in one visit gives the whole property an instant reset.
