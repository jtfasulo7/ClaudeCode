Overview.

A fresh mulch job is the fastest way to reset how a property looks. Beds get re-edged, weeds get pulled, and the mulch gets laid even — not piled up against trunks or spilling onto the lawn.

What's included

  • Hard-edge along every bed
  • Weed pull and bed-bottom prep
  • Fresh mulch laid 2–3 inches deep
  • Pulled away from tree trunks (no volcanoes)
  • Lawn and walks blown clean before we leave

Mulch types

  • Hemlock — reddish-brown, classic New England look
  • Black — high contrast against green
  • Natural — undyed, holds color longer than people expect
  • Playground — softer fall, around swing sets and play areas

Pricing.

Mulch is priced by the yard delivered + labor to install. Most residential properties in Rhode Island need 3–8 yards. Fixed-price quote after a quick walk-through — no per-hour billing.

Why it matters in Rhode Island.

Mulch does more in New England than make a bed look finished. A 2-to-3-inch layer insulates roots against our freeze-thaw winters, holds moisture through the dry midsummer stretch, and keeps weeds down during the long spring flush. Lay it too thick or pile it against trunks and you trap moisture and invite rot — the 'mulch volcanoes' you see everywhere. We edge the bed first, pull the weeds, and lay it even and pulled back from every trunk.

Frequently asked questions.

How often should mulch be refreshed?

Once a year is typical in Rhode Island — a fresh top-up each spring restores the color and keeps the depth right. Beds that lost a lot over winter may need a little more.

How much mulch will my property need?

Most residential properties take 3 to 8 yards. We measure your beds and give you a fixed price for material plus install — no per-hour billing.

What kind of mulch is best?

Hemlock gives the classic New England reddish-brown; black mulch reads sharp against green lawns; natural undyed holds its color longer than people expect. It's your call.
