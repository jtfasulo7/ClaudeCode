PHASE 3: PSYCHOLOGY-DRIVEN ANGLE SELECTION
========================================

STRATEGIC RATIONALE:

LumiRecover's unique positioning is the PRICE DISRUPTION play. At $69.99–$149.99 vs competitors at $349–$435, the value gap is massive. This is our weapon.

However, pure price-comparison burns out fast (under 90 days). So we LAYER the price advantage inside a more durable creative framework.

PRIMARY ANGLE: #3 — Us vs Them (Comparison) + #8 UGC Authentic Discovery (HYBRID)

Rationale: The price gap ($149.99 vs $399) is too powerful to waste on a pure promo ad. Instead, we embed it inside a UGC discovery narrative: a relatable woman finds the affordable alternative to overpriced masks, tests it, shows results, and validates the switch. This combines the #1 performing format (UGC demo, 36.8%) with the most potent emotional lever for this specific product (savings superiority).

PRIMARY EMOTION: Vanity/Self-Image + Instant Gratification (savings dopamine)

The target customer (women 28-48) is spending on serums, SPF, and considering clinic facials. She WANTS the results. She's TIRED of the price. We give her permission to have both: pro-level results at an accessible price. The dopamine hit comes from TWO sources: (1) "my skin looks amazing" and (2) "and I saved $300."

CIALDINI PERSUASION STACK (4 principles):

1. SOCIAL PROOF — "Join 50,000+ women who switched from $400 masks"
   Deploy via: User count, star ratings, UGC testimonials, "trending" language

2. AUTHORITY — "Same red light wavelengths used in $500 clinic treatments"
   Deploy via: Wavelength specs (630nm/660nm/850nm), FDA mention, clinical study references, dermatologist language

3. SCARCITY — "Introductory pricing won't last"
   Deploy via: Launch pricing framing, limited-time bundle offers, stock indicators

4. LIKING — Relatable creator who mirrors the target customer
   Deploy via: Woman 30-40, real skin, warm personality, "I was skeptical too" energy

HOOK ARCHETYPE: Bold/Contrarian Claim + Social Proof Lead (hybrid)

Primary hook: "I found a $69 version of those $400 LED masks everyone's obsessed with"

Backup hooks:
- "Dermatologists don't want you paying $400 for this"
- "Stop wasting money on expensive LED masks. This $99 one uses the same wavelengths."
- "POV: You discover your $400 LED mask has a $69 twin"

5-POINT CONVERSION CHECKLIST:

[X] STOP — The glowing LED mask visual + price disruption hook stops the scroll.
    The mask glow is the #1 performing visual format in this category ("mask selfie").
    Price contrast ($69 vs $400) creates instant pattern interrupt.

[X] FEEL — Triggers vanity (skin transformation desire) + savings dopamine +
    relief (finally an affordable option) + belonging (join the movement).

[X] BELIEVE — Authority stack: wavelength specs, clinical references, before/after
    proof, star ratings. Competitor price anchoring validates the technology
    ("if a $400 mask works, so does this — same wavelengths").

[X] WANT — Before/after visuals + lifestyle imagery show her future self.
    Creator-led demo makes usage tangible: "I use it for 10 minutes while
    watching TV." Low friction, high reward mental model.

[X] ACT — Clear CTA: "Shop Now" or "Get Yours for $69.99"
    Scarcity element: introductory/launch pricing.
    Direct to PDP (61% of winning beauty ads do this).
    3 price tiers give every buyer an entry point.
