№ 02  —  The landscape

The recruiting game has changed. The rules families play by haven’t.

Showcase inflation.

Thousands of athletes now attend the same combines. Being seen is no longer the same as being chosen. Film, context, and an honest evaluation are what move the needle.

Highlight-reel fatigue.

Coaches watch clips in three-second decisions. A reel without a story, without a target list, without a trusted voice attached — it lives in a thousand unread inboxes.

Noise, not signal.

AI platforms and mass-email services treat recruiting like a volume game. College coaches treat it like a relationship. The bridge between those two worlds is what we do.

№ 03  —  The founder

From Youngstown to the NCAA.

Kiyoshi “Terrell” Fish was raised in Youngstown, Ohio — one of fourteen siblings in a house built on a nearly fifty-year marriage. That’s not color; that’s the operating system. Big families teach you how to see the whole room. How to advocate for someone quieter than you. How to tell the difference between a real opportunity and a promise that won’t cash.

Fifteen years in sport evaluation followed: NFL scouting that helped surface names like Curtis Marsh and Spencer Paysinger, sideline work as a quarterback and wide-receiver coach, and a state-championship culture he helped build from inside the program. The eye was trained the long way.

Then came the part most recruiters don’t have. A Master’s in Psychology from the University of Phoenix. A parallel career as a social worker with the Department of Child Safety — ten years of advocating for young people whose futures depended on somebody in the room paying attention. That is the work. Recruiting is the same work, translated to film rooms and offer letters.

Today he works independently out of Phoenix, Arizona. No university payroll. No conference allegiances. No quota to hit. His job is the family in front of him, and the college coach he’s about to pick up the phone and call.

№ 04  —  The method

Six stages. No shortcuts.

  1. I.

    Evaluation

    An honest, scout-level assessment of your athlete’s ceiling, fit, and realistic division range. We tell you what we see — not what you want to hear.

  2. II.

    Positioning

    A profile, a film, and a narrative built the way coaches actually consume them. Bio, stats, GPA, test scores, character — every detail in its correct place.

  3. III.

    Targeting

    A custom list of programs built across three fits: division fit, academic fit, coach fit. From over 52,000 college coaches, we shortlist the ones who’d actually want you.

  4. IV.

    Outreach

    Coach-to-coach conversations, not mass emails. The introduction your athlete can’t make for themselves — made on their behalf by someone coaches already know.

  5. V.

    Navigation

    NCAA and NAIA eligibility. Camp and showcase strategy. Official visits, unofficial visits, and the hundred small decisions that add up to an offer.

  6. VI.

    Commitment

    Scholarship strategy. Financial aid review. Letter-of-intent walkthrough. We stay in the room until the right decision is signed and celebrated.

№ 07  —  Questions

Answered honestly.

When should we start?
Ideally in the sophomore or early junior year of high school. Earlier is better for national-caliber athletes. Later is still workable — we’ve placed seniors deep into the spring cycle.
Which sports do you actually cover?
Every sport. Football, basketball, baseball, softball, soccer, volleyball, lacrosse, track, swimming, wrestling, tennis, golf — men’s and women’s. If there’s a college coach recruiting it, we can reach them.
Which divisions?
NCAA Division I, II, III, NAIA, and JUCO. The right division is the one where your athlete will actually play, develop, and graduate — not the one with the biggest logo.
How is this different from a recruiting platform?
Platforms hand you a database and a template. We do the phone call. The introductions are made by a human coaches already know — that’s the entire difference.
Do you work with parents, athletes, or both?
Both, as a unit. Parents handle logistics and finances. Athletes handle training and decisions. We translate between the two and keep everyone moving in the same direction.
Is a scholarship guaranteed?
No — and be wary of anyone who tells you otherwise. What’s guaranteed is honest evaluation, direct coach access, and a process built to maximize every real opportunity.
