About this service

Additions look simple from outside. They aren't. You're tying new structure into existing in a way that has to look intentional, hold up structurally, pass code, and not blow up the rest of your house in the process.

Done well, an addition gives you the home you want without the cost or timeline of starting over. Done poorly, it ages your house ten years overnight. We do them well.

What's included

  • Structural assessment of existing home
  • Architectural design that respects the existing
  • Foundation tie-in and excavation
  • Framing match (line, profile, materials)
  • Roof tie-in and weatherproofing
  • Exterior continuity — siding, trim, paint
  • MEP extension (plumbing, electric, HVAC)
  • Interior trim and finish integration
  • Punch list and walkthrough

The honest take

Most additions take three to six months once permits are pulled. The slowest part is usually permits in your jurisdiction, not construction. We pull them on your behalf and keep you informed at every step.
